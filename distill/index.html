LOOMUS · Distill

A seven-pillar framework for distilling knowledge that compounds.

Distill is not a model — it's a mechanism that makes knowledge denser through use. Its job is not to store knowledge, but to distill scattered signals, feedback, and disagreements — through seven interlocking gears — into reusable cognitive assets.

And distillation is never the destination — it's the launchpad for connection. The point is to answer curiosity fast, make the next direction visible, and feed that clarity back into more creation. What Distill really does is turn curiosity → knowledge → creation into a loop that compounds.

Note: "distillation" here is meant in the cognitive-philosophical sense — not the teacher-student model distillation in machine learning.

The Seven Pillars

Pillar Lineage Role in Distill
I Network Value Metcalfe (N²) · Briscoe (N log N) · Reed (2^N) Every distillation aims at new connections, not new entries.
II Bayesian Inference Bayes (1763) · Jaynes (2003) ★ "Knowing" is not boolean — it is a distribution with confidence.
III Multi-Armed Bandit Thompson · UCB · Lattimore-Szepesvári Allocate exploration budget by probabilistic optimality.
IV Reinforcement Learning Sutton & Barto ★ · Christiano RLHF ★ · InstructGPT Feedback is fuel. Distillation must close the loop.
V Falsifiability Popper (1934) ★ The engine's immune system — it filters pseudo-knowledge.
VI Compounding Solow Growth · Ohno Kaizen Time is the lever. Small steps, no stopping.
VII Direct Applications Constitutional AI ★ · ReST Engineering substrate: principles before content, self-distillation loops.

Compositional Intuition

Distill = (Jaynesian updating) × (Bandit exploration) × (RLHF feedback) × (Popperian filtering) × (Kaizen compounding), running on a (Metcalfe network), with (CAI / ReST) as the engineering substrate.

Read as "in joint operation" — a metaphor for how the forces interlock, not literal arithmetic.

Seven Operating Principles

  1. Every claim must be falsifiable. Non-falsifiable items are tagged as "belief" and quarantined.
  2. Distill connections, not just content. Measure output by edges added, not nodes added.
  3. Prioritize via Thompson sampling. "What is worth distilling" is itself a bandit problem.
  4. Feedback must close the loop. Output without a return channel = broadcast — it never enters the compounding cycle.
  5. Small steps, no stopping. Kaizen beats big bang.
  6. Confidence must be explicit. Asserting without probability — i.e. defaulting to P=1.0 — is an anti-pattern.
  7. Principles before content. Set the constitution first; distill content second.
